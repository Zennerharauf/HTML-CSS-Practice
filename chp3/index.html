Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

A timeless dessert. These chocolate chip cookies are chewy, buttery, and full of chocolatey goodness.
Perfect for any occasion!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted softened butter,
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
    • Use an electric mixer on medium speed for best results.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually blend the dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
    • Mix on low speed to avoid overworking the dough.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed.
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto an ungreased baking sheet.
    • Leave about 2 inches of space between each cookie.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly browned.
  9. Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 2 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips and Ingredient Details

Unsalted Butter
Always use unsalted butter in baking to control the amount of salt in your recipe.
Brown Sugar
Brown sugar gives the cookies their chewy texture and deeper flavor.
Baking Soda
Baking soda helps the cookies rise and gives them a slightly puffy texture.
Semisweet Chocolate Chips
You can substitute with dark or milk chocolate chips based on your preference.
Don't Overmix the Dough
Overmixing the dough can lead to tough cookies. Mix just until the ingredients are combined.
Chilling the Dough ( tottly Optional)
For thicker cookies, chill the dough in the refrigerator for 30 minutes before baking.
